# Blog Post Outline (Input)

This is the raw outline used to draft the blog post. The outline defines the structure,
target length (~1000-1200 words), key arguments, and section-by-section plan.

## Structure
1. Opening Hook (100 words) -- concrete incident, thesis statement
2. The Problem with Manual Review (150 words) -- why spot-checks fail
3. Enter LLM-as-Judge (150 words) -- G-Eval mechanism
4. Four Rubrics That Actually Work (200 words) -- domain-specific rubric design
5. What Surprised Me (150 words) -- calibration, inter-model agreement, edge cases
6. What Failed and Why (150 words) -- honest accounting of failures
7. Practical Advice (150 words) -- 5 concrete recommendations
8. Closing (100 words) -- call to action

## Target Audience
AI engineers and tech leads building LLM applications in regulated domains
(healthcare, finance, legal), especially those implementing evaluation frameworks.
